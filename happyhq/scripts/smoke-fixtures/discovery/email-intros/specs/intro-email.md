# Intro Email

A warm, two-way email that introduces two people to each other and proposes a
clear next step. Lives at `outputs/intro.md`.

## Required sections

The email must include all of the following, in order:

1. **Salutation** — addresses both parties by first name (e.g. "Hi Maya, hi
   Theo,").
2. **Why I'm introducing you** — one or two sentences. The reason should make
   each party want to keep reading. Not "thought you two should meet" — what
   specifically do they each get out of this.
3. **One-line per person** — a single sentence that tells the other party
   something concrete and memorable about them. "Software engineer at X" is
   too thin; "Software engineer who spent the last two years rebuilding ACH at
   a fintech" is the bar.
4. **Personal anecdote** — at least one sentence that grounds the intro in
   real history. How I met one of them, a specific project they shipped, a
   conversation we had recently. This is the line that turns a form letter
   into a personal note. **Non-negotiable.** If the task description doesn't
   carry one and the inputs don't either, ask the user during discovery.
5. **Call to action** — a concrete next step. "I'll let you two take it from
   here" works, but a specific suggestion ("Maya, would a 20-minute call next
   week make sense?") is better when the introducer has a hunch.

## Tone

Warm but professional. First-person from the introducer. Plain English — no
corporate jargon, no buzzwords, no exclamation marks except in the salutation.
The reader should feel that a human took five minutes to write this, not that
a template generated it.

## Length

120–220 words. Anything shorter and the one-liners feel thin; anything longer
and the recipients skim past the call to action.

## Banned words and phrases

Never use: "synergy", "circle back", "touch base", "leverage" (as a verb),
"in the loop", "best in class", "rockstar", "ninja". These signal a template.

## Inputs

| Input                                       | Source         |
| ------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| Both parties' full names                    | the introducer |
| Why the introducer is connecting them       | the introducer |
| Each party's role / what they're working on | the introducer |
| Personal anecdote about at least one party  | the introducer |
