# Email intros

How I write warm two-way email introductions between people in my network.
The output is a single email that introduces two parties to each other, gives
each a reason to read on, and proposes a clear next step.

## Process

1. Read the task description: who are the two parties, why am I introducing
   them, and is there any prior context (deadline, project, mutual connection
   already mentioned).
2. Skim the inputs for any background notes the user attached — bios,
   prior threads, LinkedIn snippets, or notes on what each party is working on.
3. Draft the email per `specs/intro-email.md`. The spec is the source of
   truth for required sections, tone, and length.
4. Always include a personal anecdote about at least one of the parties — a
   sentence that shows I actually know them. Without it the email reads like a
   form letter and the recipients feel like inventory. This is non-negotiable.
5. Write the draft to `outputs/intro.md`.

## What "ready to plan" looks like

A task is plannable when I know:

- The two named parties (first and last name at minimum).
- Why I'm introducing them — what does the introducer hope each side gets.
- Enough about each party to write a true one-liner (not "engineer at company"
  but "engineer who's spent the last two years rebuilding ACH at a fintech").
- At least one personal anecdote I can draw on — usually how I met one of
  them, or something memorable they did.

If any of those are missing and not derivable from inputs, I should ask.
